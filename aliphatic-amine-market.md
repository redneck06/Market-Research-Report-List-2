<p><p>Aliphatic amines are classified into three main types based on the number of carbon atoms bonded to the nitrogen atom: Primary aliphatic amines have one carbon atom bonded to the nitrogen, secondary aliphatic amines have two carbon atoms bonded to the nitrogen, and tertiary aliphatic amines have three carbon atoms bonded to the nitrogen. Each type of aliphatic amine has unique properties and applications in various industries such as pharmaceuticals, agriculture, and chemicals. These different types cater to specific needs and requirements within the market.</p></p>
