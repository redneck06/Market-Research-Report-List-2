<p><p>Electroencephalography (EEG) biosensors are used to measure brain activity. There are different types of EEG biosensors based on the type of electrode-skin interface used. Dry EEG sensors do not require conductive gel, making them more convenient and comfortable for the user. Semi-dry EEG sensors use a minimal amount of gel, while wet EEG sensors require a significant amount of gel for proper conductivity. Other types of EEG biosensors may include wireless or miniature devices designed for specific applications or populations.</p></p>
