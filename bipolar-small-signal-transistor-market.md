<p><p>Bipolar small signal transistors are classified into two main types: PNP and NPN. PNP transistors have a positive-negative-positive configuration, where the transistor is turned on when the base is made negative with respect to the emitter. NPN transistors, on the other hand, have a negative-positive-negative configuration and are turned on when the base is made positive with respect to the emitter. Both PNP and NPN transistors are widely used in electronic circuits for amplification and switching purposes.</p></p>
