<p><p>Polyisoprene rubber is produced using various catalytic systems such as lithium catalytic, titanium catalytic, and rare earth catalysis. Lithium catalytic systems are known for their ability to produce high cis-1,4 content rubber with excellent mechanical properties. Titanium catalytic systems are preferred for their high resistance to heat and chemicals. Rare earth catalysis is utilized for its ability to produce rubbers with high molecular weight and good elasticity. Each type of catalytic system offers unique advantages and is chosen based on the desired properties of the polyisoprene rubber.</p></p>
