<p><p>Sodium dihydrogen phosphate is available in two main market types: anhydrous and dihydrate. Anhydrous sodium dihydrogen phosphate is in a solid state with no water content, while dihydrate sodium dihydrogen phosphate contains two water molecules per phosphate molecule. The anhydrous form is commonly used in industrial applications due to its stability and lower cost, while the dihydrate form is more commonly used in pharmaceuticals and food products due to its higher solubility and ease of handling.</p></p>
