<p><p>Broadband data cards are devices that allow users to connect to the internet wirelessly through cellular networks. There are two main types of markets for broadband data cards: GSM and CDMA. GSM (Global System for Mobile Communications) is used by carriers such as AT&T and T-Mobile, while CDMA (Code Division Multiple Access) is utilized by carriers like Verizon and Sprint. Both types of data cards offer high-speed internet access, but their compatibility with different networks can vary depending on the market type.</p></p>
