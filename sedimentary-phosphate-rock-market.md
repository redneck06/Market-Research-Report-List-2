<p><p>Sedimentary phosphate rock markets can be categorized into three main types: marine phosphate deposits, igneous phosphate deposits, and others. Marine phosphate deposits are formed from the accumulation of phosphate-rich sediments in marine environments, while igneous phosphate deposits are formed from the crystallization of magma rich in phosphate minerals. Other types of phosphate deposits include those formed from weathering of pre-existing rocks or through biogenic processes. Each type of phosphate deposit has unique geological origins and characteristics, impacting their market value and potential uses.</p></p>
