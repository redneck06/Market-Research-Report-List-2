<p><p>Pumped energy storage, whether utilizing seawater or freshwater, involves storing energy by pumping water uphill to a reservoir during times of low energy demand. When electricity is needed, the water is released downhill, spinning turbines that generate electricity. Seawater pumped storage is suitable for coastal regions where access to seawater is readily available, while freshwater pumped storage is more common in inland areas. Both types of pumped storage provide an efficient and reliable way to store and generate electricity when it is needed most.</p></p>
