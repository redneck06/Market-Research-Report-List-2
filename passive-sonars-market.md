<p><p>Passive sonars are a type of sonar system that detects underwater objects without emitting any signal of their own. These sonars rely on listening to the sounds emitted by other objects, such as marine vessels or marine life, to detect and track them. Passive sonars are often used in military applications for detecting enemy submarines, as well as in commercial applications for underwater surveillance and monitoring.</p><p>The passive sonars market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13.1% during the forecast period. The increasing demand for advanced underwater surveillance systems in the military sector is driving the growth of the passive sonars market. Additionally, the rising investments in underwater security and surveillance systems by governments and other organizations are further fueling the market growth.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the passive sonars market is the integration of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning technologies to enhance the capabilities of passive sonar systems. These technologies enable passive sonars to automatically analyze and classify underwater acoustic signals, providing more accurate and timely information to operators. Furthermore, the development of compact and lightweight passive sonar systems is also a key trend in the market, making these systems more portable and cost-effective for various applications.</p></p>
