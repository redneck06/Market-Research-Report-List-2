<p><p>Non visible spectrum image sensors are devices used to capture images in wavelengths that are not visible to the human eye, such as infrared or ultraviolet. There are two main types of non-visible spectrum image sensors: linear image sensors and area image sensors. </p><p>Linear image sensors capture a single line of pixels at a time, suitable for applications like barcode scanning. Area image sensors capture an entire 2D image at once, making them ideal for tasks like thermal imaging or security surveillance. Both types cater to different market needs and applications within the non-visible spectrum imaging industry.</p></p>
