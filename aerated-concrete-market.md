<p><p>Aerated concrete, also known as autoclaved aerated concrete (AAC), is a lightweight precast building material made of finely ground sand, cement, water, and aluminum powder. The mixture is poured into molds and undergoes a chemical reaction that produces hydrogen gas, creating air bubbles in the material. These air bubbles give aerated concrete its lightweight and insulating properties.</p><p>The Aerated Concrete Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.8% during the forecast period. The market growth is attributed to the increasing demand for sustainable and energy-efficient construction materials. Aerated concrete is known for its thermal insulation properties, reducing energy costs for heating and cooling buildings. Moreover, its lightweight nature makes it easier and faster to install, resulting in cost savings for construction projects.</p><p>The latest trends in the aerated concrete market include the development of advanced AAC formulations with enhanced strength and durability. Manufacturers are also focusing on expanding their product offerings to cater to a wider range of construction applications.
