<p><p>Iron oxide nanomaterials are categorized into two main types: Fe2O3 and Fe3O4. Fe2O3, also known as ferric oxide or hematite, is a red-colored iron oxide that is commonly used in pigments, coatings, and polishing agents. Fe3O4, also known as magnetite, is a black-colored iron oxide that exhibits magnetic properties and is widely used in magnetic recording media, biomedical applications, and environmental remediation. Both types of iron oxide nanomaterials have unique properties and applications in various industries.</p></p>
